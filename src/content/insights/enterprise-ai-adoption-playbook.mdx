## The Pilot Graveyard

Eighty-seven percent of enterprise AI projects never make it past pilot stage. Not because the technology doesn't work. Not because the business case isn't there. But because **nobody planned for the operational reality of scaling AI across a marketing organisation.**

The pilot worked beautifully. The demo impressed the board. The ROI projections looked fantastic. Then someone asked: "Great. Now how do we roll this out to 200 marketers across 15 countries?"

Silence.

## Why Pilots Fail to Scale

### The Skills Gap
Your pilot was run by your most technically capable team member—the one person who actually understood the AI tools. Now you need that capability across the entire organisation. You can't clone people.

### The Process Gap
The pilot operated outside normal workflows. It had special permissions, dedicated time, and executive air cover. Normal operations have none of these luxuries.

### The Governance Gap
Nobody worried about brand compliance, data privacy, or quality assurance during a three-week pilot. At scale, these become existential risks.

## The Operational Playbook

### Phase 1: Capability Audit (Week 1-2)
Before deploying any AI solution at scale, audit your team's actual capabilities. Not their interest in AI. Not their attendance at webinars. Their ability to operate AI-enhanced workflows independently.

### Phase 2: Workflow Design (Week 2-4)
Design AI-enhanced workflows that integrate with existing processes—not replace them. The fastest path to adoption is making the current way of working better, not asking people to learn an entirely new system.

### Phase 3: Embedded Expertise (Week 4-8)
Deploy AI-fluent specialists who work alongside your teams. Not as trainers lecturing from slides. As operators building real campaigns together. Skills transfer happens through doing, not watching.

### Phase 4: Governance Framework (Week 6-10)
Build the compliance, quality, and brand safety frameworks that let AI operate at enterprise scale. This isn't bureaucracy—it's the infrastructure that gives leadership confidence to approve expansion.

### Phase 5: Independent Operation (Week 10-12)
The goal isn't permanent dependency on external expertise. It's building internal capability that sustains itself. Measure success by what the team can do after the specialists leave.
