# Creative Brief for Web Designer

## **Creative Brief for Web Designer**

**Core Concept:** "Agility & Scale": The site must communicate high-end business value, speed, and the elimination of corporate bureaucracy when it comes to hiring on-demand AI-fluent marketers and scaling marketing teams across regions. 

## **1\. Project Overview**

Algomarketing is a boutique Contingent Marketing Workforce provider focused on providing **AI-savvy marketing professionals to enterprise organisations**. Algomarketing exists to give enterprise marketing teams a competitive advantage by embedding AI capability directly into execution — without long hiring cycles, training delays, or operational risk. It is the structured deployment of AI-trained marketing professionals inside enterprise environments — safely, quickly, and predictably. 

Algomarketing sits at the intersection of:

* enterprise marketing execution  
* compliant workforce delivery  
* AI capability

We help marketing leaders:

* Access AI-fluent marketing talent immediately  
* Scale execution without permanent hiring  
* Reduce compliance and operational risk and mitigate talent fraud  
* Build AI capability into everyday marketing workflows

Unlike traditional staffing firms, Algomarketing doesn’t simply place talent.

We design AI-enabled delivery models that combine:

* Experienced enterprise marketers  
* Practical AI and automation expertise  
* Structured workforce management  
* Operational oversight

**The website must position Algomarketing as**:

The trusted partner for enterprises that want AI-fluent marketing talent deployed correctly — not experimentally.

**Core idea:**

We don’t sell people or hours. We provide AI-fluent enterprise marketing talent, deployed through structured delivery models that make marketing execution faster, safer, and more scalable.

---

## **2\. Website Goal**

* Clearly explain *what Algomarketing does, what its value is,* and *which business problem the company solves* within the first screen.  
* Hold the attention of **Senior Marketing Managers, Heads of Marketing, Marketing Directors and other mid-level Marketing Leaders**. The people we sell to are closely involved in day-to-day marketing work.  
* Drive users toward a clear conversion (contact / discovery call).

---

## **3\. Target Audience**

* **Primary:** Senior Marketing Managers, Heads of Marketing, and Marketing Directors, other Marketing Leadership roles.  
* Busy decision-makers in Marketing within their companies who scan content quickly and **often browse on mobile**.

---

## **4\. Mobile-First (Critical)**

* Up to **92% of our traffic comes from mobile devices**.  
* Design must be **mobile-first**, with desktop as an adaptation.  
* Vertical logic: **one core message per screen**.  
* Avoid dense tables, small text, and overloaded layouts.  
* Navigation and CTAs must be accessible and easy to understand what to do.

---

## **5\. Tone & Feel**

* Enterprise-grade B2B design. Assertive and friendly, clean and easy to read.  
* The design should communicate:  
  * speed  
  * control  
  * scalability and flexibility  
  * confidence

---

## **6\. Visual Identity**

* **Strictly follow [the brand book](https://pitch.com/v/algo-brand-style-guide-xh6bn6/e5de32d2-1795-4229-9b6a-f3b4303960d3)** (colors, typography, visual style).  
* Bold, confident typography — headlines **carry meaning**, not decoration.  
* Minimal use of boring stock photography.  
* Preference for:  
  * abstract  
  * technological  
  * system-oriented visual solutions (infographics, laconic tables)  
* Motion and micro-animations are welcome if they **enhance meaning**, not distract.

---

## **7\. Hierarchy & Content Logic**

* Design must support the structure: **Problem → Solution → Outcome → CTA**  
* Clear visual and semantic hierarchy:  
  * what matters most  
  * what is secondary  
  * where to go next  
* Each section should logically guide the user to the next step.  
* The design should integrate proof alongside the claims \+ promises made through site copy.  
  Visual hierarchy should prioritize data, outcomes, and case study elements.  
  * Examples:  
    * Citations  
    * Quotes from authority sources  
    * Client logos  
    * Measurable results  
  * Executed through section elements for:  
    * Quotes  
    * Stats  
    * Bullets  
    * Сase study briefs (with snippet \+/or embeds \+/or links to the full case study)

All these should be prominent design elements alongside the site text.

* **The site should feel like:**  
  * → The leading destination for AI-fluent enterprise marketing talent — combined with the delivery expertise required to deploy that talent effectively at scale.  
  * → Algomarketing helps enterprise marketing teams design and scale modern execution models powered by AI-enabled marketers.  
* **NOT like:**→ Stuffing, outsourcing, generic AI hype. It is not about buying access to an AI platform that happens to have some humans involved somewhere

**NOTE:** Want to still be human-centric. Yes, we're selling increased capability through custom-built AI solutions and the feel is 'marketing operating system'... But the solutions are built by **human specialists**. Want to balance the AI, systems, and futuristic elements of our brand with real operator profiles as well as case studies \+ testimonials that feature real names, faces, roles, company / team, etc

---

## **8\. Conversion & CTAs**

* **CTAs should be highly visible, but not aggressive.**  
* On mobile, it is acceptable and recommended to use a **sticky CTA in the bottom area of the screen**, ensuring the primary action is always accessible without disrupting content.  
* The sticky CTA should not cover key content and may adapt to scroll behavior (e.g., appear after the first screen).  
* Contact Form

### **Suggested Sticky CTA Copy (Mobile):**

1. **Book a Discovery Call**  
    *Direct and conversion-focused.*  
2. **Get In Touch**  
    *Softer tone, well-suited for enterprise audiences.*

(CTA copy may vary depending on page type and funnel stage.)

---

## **9\. Links & Interactive Text (Important)**

* Text that contains **hyperlinks should be visually highlighted in a creative and intentional way**, not limited to standard underlining or default link styling.  
* Linked text should clearly signal interactivity and importance, while remaining consistent with the brand system.  
* The goal is to make links **noticeable and engaging**, especially for scanning users, without breaking visual hierarchy or readability.  
* Reference [https://uamaster.net/google-ads-works-based-on-intent/](https://uamaster.net/google-ads-works-based-on-intent/) 

---

## **10\. Contact Form Requirements (Critical)**

* The contact / booking form must collect business-relevant information required for CRM and sales qualification when a user books a call.  
* Form fields should focus on business context, not just personal contact details (e.g., company name, email, phone number, industry, their role or job function within the company, team size, use case). The list of fields is to be specified later.  
* Each field must include clear placeholder hints or helper text, explaining exactly what information should be entered.  
* Required fields must be clearly marked with an asterisk (\*) and visually distinguishable. This feature should be configurable so that we can adjust the form settings and choose which specific fields must be visible and mandatory for completion.  
* The form must include anti-spam protection and first-level lead qualification mechanisms to block irrelevant or low-quality submissions (e.g., bots, non-business inquiries).  
* The overall form experience should feel enterprise-grade, intentional, and trustworthy, not generic or consumer-style.

---

## **11\. Trust & Engagement**

* Trust is built through:  
  * clear structure  
  * case studies and social proof  
  * the feel of a mature, enterprise-ready product  
* The design should leave room for future interactive elements (calculators, dynamic blocks), even if they are not implemented immediately.

---

## **12\. Scalability**

* The design ***must scale easily*** to support:  
  * new solution pages  
  * role-based pages (Marketing / Recruiting / Procurement)  
* Maintainability and clarity are more important than complex custom effects.

The redesign must clearly **differentiate Algomarketing from traditional staffing firms**. The website should communicate that we are a consultative partner that can assist them on their journey to building an AI-fluent marketing workforce via the talent we provide. We are not a generic workforce supply.

## **FAQ about Contingent Marketing Talent Solutions**

**What is a contingent marketing workforce?**  
A contingent marketing workforce is a flexible pool of marketing specialists who work on a temporary or project basis, without becoming full-time employees. This approach lets companies scale marketing execution without the overhead, delays, or compliance risks of traditional hiring.

**How is Algomarketing different from a staffing firm, agency, or freelance marketplace?**  
We're a contingent marketing workforce provider focused exclusively on marketing execution. Unlike generic staffing firms, we don't just send you resumes; we embed AI-fluent marketing operators directly into your team, manage the entire engagement, and handle payroll, compliance, and performance. And unlike agencies, we don't own the work. **Your team does.**

**Who are Algos?**  
Algos are AI-fluent marketing specialists who embed directly into your team. They're trained to execute modern marketing workflows, integrate into your systems, and deliver results fast.

**How fast can we get contingent marketing resources?**  
Most clients have temp marketers embedded and delivering within 2-3 weeks. For urgent needs, we can move even faster.

**What contingent marketing roles can you provide?**  
We provide specialists across demand gen, lifecycle marketing, RevOps, paid media, content, SEO, marketing ops, and more. If you need a marketing skill, we likely have it.

**Can you fill roles outside the listed niches?**  
Yes. Our **team augmentation services** are flexible. If you have a specific marketing need, we'll find the right specialist.

**How can I hire through Algomarketing?**  
Simple. Book a call, tell us what you're trying to execute, and we'll recommend the right contingent workforce solution. From there, we handle sourcing, onboarding, and delivery.

**Who will my company have a contractual relationship with?**  
You contract with Algomarketing. We manage the temp marketers, payroll, compliance, and performance. You focus on execution.

**How long does it take to scale my marketing team or embed contingent marketers?**  
Most engagements are live within 2-3 weeks. If you need to scale marketing faster, we can accelerate.

**Where do your contingent marketers come from?**  
Our network spans North America, Europe, Latin America, and Asia. We match you with talent based on skills, time zone, and cultural fit.

**How do contract terminations work with Algomarketing?**  
Engagements are flexible. You can scale up, scale down, or end contracts based on your needs. We handle offboarding and compliance.

**How do temp contractors get paid?**  
We handle payroll. Your temp marketers are paid by Algomarketing, not your company.

**How do you handle risk, compliance, and payroll?**  
We manage contracts, worker classification, cross-border payments, and compliance. That's part of our contingent marketing workforce management service.
